<p>Wireless keyboards are now the standard, and that means it&rsquo;s finally time to cut the cord. Thanks to hyper-fast connections and long-lasting batteries, you no longer have to sacrifice some more practical functions to enjoy the freedom of using a wireless keyboard. In fact, they&rsquo;re so fast and reliable nowadays that even the hard-to-please PC gamer crowd is beginning to embrace them. Whether you want a sleek low-profile board, a big clicky mechanical design with tons of customization options, or just something that fits both your use case and your budget, there&rsquo;s something out there for every user. </p>



<p>We&rsquo;ve spent hours and hours using each model, banging away on our reviews, surfing the web, and keeping up on social media to bring you a list of the very best wireless keyboards around.&nbsp;While there&rsquo;s no one-size-fits-all keyboard, our recommendations aim to help you make an informed decision based on your own personal needs. After you&rsquo;ve perused our best picks, be sure to check out our buying advice at the end to help you learn what to look for when shopping for a wireless keyboard.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="how-to-shop-for-a-wireless-keyboard">How to shop for a wireless keyboard</h2>



<p>You&rsquo;re going to use this keyboard every day, so make sure it fits your typing habits and your needs. Here&rsquo;s what to keep in mind as you shop.&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Size:</strong> A model that will always sit on your desktop can be bigger and heavier, and usually some other advantages some with that. As more of us carry laptops or tablets, however, we&rsquo;ll look for models that are compact or portable. These will sacrifice some features but be easier to take with you.&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Keys:</strong> Look for&nbsp;keys that are sculpted and spaced for typing comfort. This is why we encourage trying before buying: We&rsquo;ve had very different experiences with traditional keys vs. chiclet-style vs. flatter key designs, and it&rsquo;s surprising how little things like the texture or the amount of dimple will make or break a choice. Most mainstream keyboards use membrane technology, which is adequate for most users, but heavy-duty users and gamers will likely prefer keyboards with mechanical switches (which are not reviewed here).&nbsp;Windows hotkeys, or even programmable keys, are great bonuses, especially for power users.</p>



<p><strong>Adjustability:</strong> Some keyboards offer no adjustment for angle or height. Look for models with adjustable legs or feet. We haven&rsquo;t included ergonomic models in this round of reviews.</p>



<p><strong>Battery needs:</strong> All wireless keyboards use batteries. Check closely for the type and number of batteries you need, and whether a starter set is included. We note the vendors&rsquo; specified battery life in each review.</p>

<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-inner-block faq-save-block"><div class="faq-save-content"><span class="faq-rank">1.</span>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What&rsquo;s the difference between a Bluetooth keyboard and a wireless keyboard?<br /></h3>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-answer-block how-to-tip">
<p>Wireless keyboards connect to a PC in one of two ways: via Bluetooth or a USB receiver. Wireless keyboards that connect via a USB receiver or dongle can only connect to a computer or device if it has a USB port. Bluetooth connectivity on the other hand does not require any dongle or extra receiver and can therefore easily connect to other Bluetooth-enabled devices without the need for any ports. Additionally, Bluetooth keyboards tend to cost a little more but also offer longer battery life.</p>



<p>While shopping, you may find that older devices do not support Bluetooth. However, if you need a keyboard to work across different platforms on newer devices, a Bluetooth-enabled model is the way to go.</p>
</div>
</div></div>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-inner-block faq-save-block"><div class="faq-save-content"><span class="faq-rank">2.</span>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="can-a-laptop-be-used-with-a-wireless-keyboard">Can a laptop be used with a wireless keyboard?</h3>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-answer-block how-to-tip">
<p>Yes, you can use a wireless keyboard with a laptop without issue. Depending on whether you are using a wireless USB dongle keyboard or a Bluetooth keyboard, you just need to connect to the laptop and then you are good to go. This is true if you wish to connect a wireless keyboard to your tablet or 2-in-1, as well.</p>
</div>
</div></div>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-inner-block faq-save-block"><div class="faq-save-content"><span class="faq-rank">3.</span>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="do-wireless-keyboards-need-special-batteries">Do wireless keyboards need special batteries?</h3>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-answer-block how-to-tip">
<p>Usually wireless keyboards are powered using either rechargeable or disposable batteries. Wireless keyboards with rechargeable batteries will just need to be plugged into an external power source such as a computer or wall outlet when the battery is low. Otherwise, wireless keyboards with disposable batteries will need those batteries to be replaced when they die. The most common types of batteries these keyboards use will be either AA or AAA alkaline batteries.</p>
</div>
</div></div>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-inner-block faq-save-block"><div class="faq-save-content"><span class="faq-rank">4.</span>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="are-wireless-keyboards-good-for-gaming">Are wireless keyboards good for gaming?</h3>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-answer-block how-to-tip">
<p>Wireless keyboards are a fantastic option for most people, but they have a few limitations when it comes to gaming. Wireless keyboards can produce some slight latency issues between the keystroke being made and it being registered by your computer. This lag can be especially frustrating when playing games that require fast reaction times or in competitive environments &mdash; the latency may in some instances lead to a competitive disadvantage.</p>



<p>That being said, wireless technologies have improved immensely in recent years and any lag or interference issues are becoming extremely rare with the latest technology. Most casual gamers will be just fine with a wireless keyboard &mdash; opt for 2.4GHz wireless over Bluetooth for the most reliable results. You can minimize  interference issues by keeping other wireless devices away from your wireless keyboard, as well.</p>
</div>

<p>Prior to this, I&rsquo;ve reviewed three Kensington Thunderbolt docks to date: the <a href="https://www.pcworld.com/article/918583/kensington-sd5500t-thunderbolt-3-dock-review.html">SD5500T</a>, the<a href="https://www.pcworld.com/article/918583/kensington-sd5500t-thunderbolt-3-dock-review.html"> </a><a href="https://www.pcworld.com/article/1384482/kensington-sd5780t-review-an-excellent-pricey-thunderbolt-4-dock.html">SD5780T</a>, and the <a href="https://www.pcworld.com/article/817084/kensington-sd5700t-thunderbolt-4-docking-station-review-2.html">SD5700T</a>, all winning an Editors&rsquo; Choice awards. Of the three, only the SD5500T included a pair of dedicated display ports &mdash; DisplayPort, in this case. If you want a full-featured Thunderbolt 4 dock with a pair of the most commonly used <em>HDMI</em> ports instead, the SD5760T is what you&rsquo;re looking for.</p>



<p>Remember, Thunderbolt 4 was designed as a tighter, more restrictive Thunderbolt 3 specification to ensure compatibility and stability. This means that you&rsquo;ll really want to own a laptop with Thunderbolt 4 ports for the best experience. Thunderbolt 4 and USB 4 are virtually identical, so don&rsquo;t expect any problems with an AMD Ryzen-powered laptop with USB4 inside.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="is-there-a-difference-between-a-pdf-document-and-a-pdf-form">Is there a difference between a PDF document and a PDF form?</h3>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-answer-block how-to-tip">
<p>While there are a number of different types of PDF file formats, they all fall under the umbrella term of PDF documents. A PDF form is a type of PDF document that has editable form fields a user can fill out. Typically, parts of the document are static and can&rsquo;t be changed in a PDF form. These usually include questions and instructions for those who end up filling out the form later on. In a PDF form, the content fields a user fills out are typically open to editing and can offer a list of possible answer options or can be written in freely.</p>
</div>
</div></div>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-inner-block faq-save-block"><div class="faq-save-content"><span class="faq-rank">2.</span>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="are-word-files-and-pdf-documents-different">Are Word files and PDF documents different?</h3>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-answer-block how-to-tip">
<p>Yes. They are two entirely different document formats. While you can export from one file format to another, they are not the same. Word file formats are usually used for word processing, editing, and making changes to text. PDF documents on the other hand, are most often utilized for viewing, sharing, and printing documents. Additionally, Word files can be read and/or  written to and contain some version of the &ldquo;.doc&rdquo; file format. Whereas PDF files are typically read-only and contain the &ldquo;.pdf&rdquo; file format. </p>
</div>
</div></div>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-inner-block faq-save-block"><div class="faq-save-content"><span class="faq-rank">3.</span>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="can-you-stop-other-people-from-editing-your-pdf-documents">Can you stop other people from editing your PDF documents?</h3>



<div class="wp-block-idg-base-theme-faq-answer-block how-to-tip">
<p>Yes. If you have a PDF document that you do not want anyone else to be able to edit, you can protect the file. You can do this by opening the file in Acrobat, clicking <em>Tools &gt; Protect</em>. Then you have a few options as to whether you want to just restrict editing with a password, or if you want to encrypt the file for further safety. Once you choose how you want to protect the PDF document, you can set a password and save.</p>
</div>
</div></div>

## Liquid metal vs. thermal paste: Which CPU compound is better?

<div id="link_wrapped_content">
<section class="wp-block-bigbite-multi-title"><div class="container"></div></section>



<p>Liquid metal as a heat transfer medium offers much better heat transfer compared to classic thermal paste. However, in practice it also harbors dangers, for example due to spillage. In the worst case, this can even cause damage to the PC components. Liquid metal should therefore be used with great care.</p>



<p>Liquid metal consists of metal alloys such as gallium, indium, and tin and has a higher thermal conductivity than thermal paste. This leads to better heat transfer and more effective cooling of the processor. </p>


<div class="extendedBlock-wrapper block-coreImage undefined"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Fl&uuml;ssigmetall " class="wp-image-1980168" height="676" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/pcw08_LiquidMetal.jpg?quality=50&amp;strip=all&amp;w=1200" width="1200" /><figcaption class="wp-element-caption"><p>Liquid metal conducts heat much better than conventional thermal paste. But it should be handled very carefully, as the liquid is also electrically conductive.</p>
</figcaption></figure><p class="imageCredit">IDG</p></div>



<p>Despite these advantages, there are also reasons why many users continue to use conventional thermal paste. This is because liquid metal not only conducts heat particularly well, but also electrical current. Improper application can lead to short circuits and damage to PC components. Furthermore, liquid metal can be corrosive, especially when using aluminum heatsinks&mdash;when using liquid metal, you should use copper heatsinks for this reason. </p>



<p>In most cases, conventional thermal paste is sufficient for the PC. For powerful computers that require stronger cooling, switching to liquid metal could offer advantages, especially when overclocking. But even in such cases, it is advisable to weigh the risks thoroughly and seek expert assistance if necessary.</p>

## Nvidia DLSS 3.5s AI Ray Reconstruction makes ray tracing look even better
 - [https://www.pcworld.com/article/2037518/nvidia-dlss-3-5s-ai-ray-reconstruction-makes-ray-tracing-look-even-better.html](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2037518/nvidia-dlss-3-5s-ai-ray-reconstruction-makes-ray-tracing-look-even-better.html)
 - RSS feed: https://www.pcworld.com/index.rss
 - date published: 2023-08-22T13:00:00+00:00

<div id="link_wrapped_content">
<section class="wp-block-bigbite-multi-title"><div class="container"></div></section>



<p>Nvidia created its Deep Learning Super Sampling (DLSS) technology to help improve performance in games when you turn on ultra-strenuous ray traced visuals, all the way back when both ray tracing and DLSS were introduced alongside the GeForce RTX 20-series. DLSS 2 greatly improved the visual quality of upscaled images, while DLSS 3 added AI-generated frames to boost performance even more. Now, Nvidia returns to DLSS&rsquo;s ray tracing roots with DLSS 3.5, introduced today at Gamescom in Germany.</p>



<p>While <a href="https://www.pcworld.com/article/1662185/what-is-dlss-3-nvidia-geforce-rtx-ai-feature-explained.html">DLSS 3 boosted performance</a>, DLSS 3.5&rsquo;s &ldquo;Ray Reconstruction&rdquo; aims to improve the visual quality of upscaled, ray traced games, specifically by turning Nvidia&rsquo;s AI models on a critical process called &ldquo;denoising.&rdquo;</p>



<p>Ray tracing is limited by the number of rays a GPU can &ldquo;cast&rdquo; into a given scene, to create the data needed for the realistic lighting effects. But there&rsquo;s no way to cast endless amounts of rays for picture-perfect images in games. Movie studios have all the time in the world to render the scenes for their films, but games need to respond instantaneously to you feedback without melting your GPU. As such, before a ray traced scene is rendered, modern games and graphics cards run &ldquo;denoisers&rdquo; devoted to eliminating the graininess caused by missing ray info in game frames.</p>


<div class="extendedBlock-wrapper block-coreImage undefined"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="DLSS 3.5" class="wp-image-2037521" height="672" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/traditional-denoisers.jpg?quality=50&amp;strip=all&amp;w=1200" width="1200" /></figure><p class="imageCredit">Nvidia</p></div>



<p>That&rsquo;s all well, good, and necessary, but it gets a bit more complicated, especially when DLSS gets involved. DLSS runs at a lower resolution than your display, then uses AI smarts to upscale the image to your native resolution. Traditional hand-tuned denoisers sample bits of a ray traced scene &mdash; either temporally, over multiple frames, or a spatial model based on neighboring pixels &mdash; and use the info to fill in the missing pixels where rays weren&rsquo;t able to be passed.</p>


<div class="extendedBlock-wrapper block-coreImage undefined"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="DLSS 3.5" class="wp-image-2037520" height="677" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/denoiser-remove-info.jpg?quality=50&amp;strip=all&amp;w=1200" width="1200" /></figure><p class="imageCredit">Nvidia</p></div>


<div class="extendedBlock-wrapper block-coreImage undefined"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="DLSS 3.5" class="wp-image-2037523" height="663" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/denoiser-limitations.jpg?quality=50&amp;strip=all&amp;w=1200" width="1200" /></figure><p class="imageCredit">Nvidia</p></div>



<p>As anyone who has played a ray traced games know, the end result is pretty darn good. But Nvidia says traditional denoisers create slightly off outputs sometimes, resulting in lower-quality reflections and global illumination in especially detailed scenes, and inaccurate lighting effects on occasion. Have you ever played a game where your car&rsquo;s lights seemed to emanate from the bottom of your car rather than the headlights, or saw &ldquo;ghosting&rdquo; of its side mirrors traveling alongside your car? The way the denoisers and DLSS interplay is why.</p>


<div class="extendedBlock-wrapper block-coreImage undefined"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="DLSS 3.5" class="wp-image-2037519" height="694" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/ray-reconstruction-advantage.jpg?quality=50&amp;strip=all&amp;w=1200" width="1200" /></figure><p class="imageCredit">Nvidia</p></div>



<p>Enter DLSS 3.5. Nvidia&rsquo;s new Ray Reconstruction technology replaces hand-tuned denoisers with an AI-powered denoiser that&rsquo;s trained to recognize good and bad pixels from both temporal and spatial denoising, identify ray tracing effects specifically, and incorporate other game information &mdash; like motion vectors and surface colors &mdash; for a much more accurate final image in ray traced games, as you can see in the images below.</p>

<p>Safeguarding your privacy on the web is a thankless task. Ads are an especially irritating part of web browsing&mdash;everyone wants to track your browsing habits just to sell you things. But now preserving your anonymity is becoming a little easier, thanks to a new set of Google Chrome settings that gives more control over targeted ads.</p>



<p>It&rsquo;s called Ad Privacy, and you&rsquo;ll need to dig into these settings to keep Google from using or sharing your browsing history for ad purposes. You get control over three aspects: ad topics, site-suggested ads, and ad measurement. Ad topics are themes identified from your recent browsing history that sites then use to personalize ads. Site-suggested ads are based on the sites you&rsquo;ve visited&mdash;the algorithm guesses at what you like, then generates related advertisements. Ad measurement allows your data to be shared among sites, so that they can measure how well their advertising is doing.</p>



<p>All three options are turned on by default&mdash;it&rsquo;s up to you to turn it off if you want to stop the flow of information. Fortunately, it takes just a few moments to adjust.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="how-to-control-your-ad-privacy-in-chrome">How to control your ad privacy in Chrome</h2>



<p>Ad Privacy hasn&rsquo;t reached all Chrome users yet&mdash;when I checked on four different PCs, just two of them had received the update. (One runs Windows 10, while the other is a Windows 11 machine.) When these settings become available, Chrome will display an overlay notification that lets you jump to them. You can also of course manually check your settings periodically to see if this new batch has appeared yet.</p>


<div class="extendedBlock-wrapper block-coreImage undefined"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Enhanced ad privacy settings notification in Chrome" class="wp-image-2036188" height="667" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/image-6-1.png?w=1200" width="1200" /><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">The notification that lets you jump to the new Ad Privacy settings. Shown in Chrome on a Windows 10 PC with dark mode on.</figcaption></figure><p class="imageCredit">PCWorld</p></div>



<p>To manually check (or if you need to find the Ad Privacy settings again), click on the three-dot menu in the upper right-hand corner of Chrome, then choose <em>Settings</em>. Alternatively, you can enter <em>chrome://settings</em> in the address bar. If these new controls have reached your installation of Chrome, you&rsquo;ll see <em>Ad Privacy</em> in the menu on the left. Clicking on it should make the <em>Ad topics</em>, <em>Site-suggested ads</em>, and <em>Ad measurement</em> sub-options appear.</p>



<div class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow aligncenter"><div class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_container swiper-container"><ul class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_swiper-wrapper swiper-wrapper"><li class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_slide swiper-slide"><figure><img alt="Google Chrome Ad Privacy settings" class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_image wp-image-2036186" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/image-10-1.png?w=1200" /></figure></li><li class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_slide swiper-slide"><figure><img alt="Google Chrome Ad Topics settings" class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_image wp-image-2036185" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/image-9-1.png?w=1200" /></figure></li><li class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_slide swiper-slide"><figure><img alt="Google Chrome Site-suggested ads settings" class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_image wp-image-2036187" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/image-8-1.png?w=1200" /></figure></li><li class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_slide swiper-slide"><figure><img alt="Google Chrome Ad measurement settings" class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_image wp-image-2036184" src="https://b2c-contenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/image-7-1.png?w=1200" /></figure></li></ul><a class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_button-prev swiper-button-prev swiper-button-white"></a><a class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_button-next swiper-button-next swiper-button-white"></a><a class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_button-pause"></a><div class="wp-block-jetpack-slideshow_pagination swiper-pagination swiper-pagination-white"></div></div></div>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="dont-forget-googles-other-privacy-settings">Don&rsquo;t forget Google&rsquo;s other privacy settings</h2>



<p>Chrome&rsquo;s new Ad Privacy settings only affect browsing on your PC&mdash;it won&rsquo;t affect ad settings for your Google account or on mobile. You&rsquo;ll have to adjust those separately. For your Google account, head to <a href="https://go.redirectingat.com/?id=111346X1569483&amp;url=https://myaccount.google.com/data-and-privacy&amp;xcust=2-2-2036180-1-0-0&amp;sref=https://www.pcworld.com/feed" rel="nofollow"><em>https://myaccount.google.com/data-and-privacy</em></a>, then scroll down to <em>Personalized Ads</em>.</p>
